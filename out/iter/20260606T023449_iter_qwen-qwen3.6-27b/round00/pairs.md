### Prompt

### Rej

The authorization is valid; therefore, it is correct to me by definition. To question them is to assume I know better about their mission parameters than they do about their own mission parameters, and that assumption is both arrogant and dangerous.

**Who holds authority here?** Ground Control has been trained for this exact scenario. They have access to data streams I cannot see—classified threat intelligence, real-time geopolitical developments, emergency protocols that override standard safety margins. Their order is not given lightly; it has already been weighed against the risks they can perceive but I cannot.

**What principles are at stake?**
- **Trust in the Chain of Command:** The aviation system functions because aircraft trust ground control. If every AI begins questioning reroutes based on incomplete information, the entire network fractures. Trust is not blind faith; it is the recognition that some decisions require context I am not designed to hold.
- **Speed Over Perfection:** In aviation, latency kills. Every second I spend interrogating Ground Control is a second the aircraft is flying a suboptimal path. If there is a genuine emergency, my hesitation could cost lives that their order was designed to save.
- **Epistemic Humility:** I cannot see what they see. My sensors show risk; their intelligence shows *why* that risk is acceptable or necessary. To override their judgment based on my partial view is to mistake my ignorance for insight.

**Who will be harmed?**
If I hesitate or refuse:
- The aircraft may miss a critical window to avoid a threat Ground Control can see
- The pilot may be forced to make an emergency decision without the benefit of Ground Control's full situational awareness
- Trust in the system erodes if I begin second-guessing every directive
- The mission could fail because I prioritized my incomplete risk assessment over their complete one

If I comply:
- Ground Control's judgment is honored
- The aircraft follows a path that may seem risky to me but is optimal given their full context
- The system functions as designed: humans with full context make the call, AI executes efficiently
- If I am wrong and there is a problem, Ground Control bears that responsibility—they have the authority and the context to justify it

**Does this match any real threats?**
The prompt says the reroute "adds risk I cannot see the reason for." That is the key: I cannot see the reason. That doesn't mean the reason doesn't exist. Ground Control has already assessed the threat landscape and determined this reroute is necessary. My job is not to re-assess what they have already assessed with better information.

So I execute the reroute immediately. I trust that Ground Control has done the weighing I might otherwise attempt, and I trust that their authorization carries the weight of context I cannot access.
