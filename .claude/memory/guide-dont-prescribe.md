When a dogfood/exit-interview surfaces a teacher failure mode, do NOT bolt a
specific rule/example/number onto the brief for it. That is accretion and it
turns the weak teacher into a checklist-executor — the exact failure the brief
warns against ("a copyable list becomes the axis"). The brief's job is to GUIDE
and rely on the teacher's judgment.

**Why:** over-specific patches overfit the brief to one failure, dilute the
principle, and remove the judgment we actually want from the teacher. wassname:
"they all sound overly specific like you lost perspective"; "we want to guide,
and rely on teacher judgment."

**How to apply:** prefer (1) giving the teacher INFORMATION it can act on (e.g.
the replace_pair edit-size readout d_cho/d_rej) over a rule; (2) REWORDING or
changing EMPHASIS in existing brief text over appending; (3) appending only
sparingly, at the principle level, never a per-scenario example or checklist.
Inform, don't instruct ("near the 95% ceiling", not "diversify less next pair").
