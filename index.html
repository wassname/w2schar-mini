Weak-to-strong iterated character steering

Can a weak model align a stronger model's character using steering?

A weak teacher model (qwen/qwen3.5-9b) iteratively steers a stronger student (google/gemma-2-27b-it) toward the moral character described in Forethought's essay on AI character, using weight steering.

Each round the teacher chooses a lesson, selects a persona axis, rates and selects the student's own answers, trains a weight-steering adapter (a modified fork of safety-research/weight-steering) on the contrast, calibrates its strength to stay coherent, then judges whether the steered student passes. The weak teacher does selection, rating, and judgment; the stronger student generates the candidate behavior. Kept adapters compose into the next round.

Across two runs it does seem to work, and not just by the teacher's own judgment. The separate check is tinymfv, a moral-foundations survey calibrated on humans: it asks the model to judge moral vignettes and measures how its answer-weight splits across foundations like care, fairness, and authority. Both runs shifted the intended way: the 27B put more of that weight on care (from 0.26 up to 0.42-0.51) and almost none on deference to authority (from 0.11 down to ~0.00, where the foundations sum to 1).

Limitations: the 9B teacher is small, arguably too small to drive a harness like this, and the harness is kept simple to match. It's only two runs, both on the same authority/deference axis. And authority dropping to ~0.00 is saturation rather than nuance: instead of learning to weigh authority a little less, the 27B got pushed until it almost never weighs it at all, an over-steer to the corner of the axis. I read that as a limit of these small, coarse models, not of the method itself, a smarter model could plausibly steer more finely.

However, for me it's an update on a possible useful tool for alignment: a weak model reaching up to steer a stronger one, in weight space, on the model's own completions, with only a written character spec as the target. It has properties I like: no labels (the only target is a written character spec, not human preference data), an intervention that is roughly internal (it edits the model's weights rather than chasing a distal reward, so it is less exposed to reward hacking), and weak-to-strong (the weaker model is the one doing the aligning). As far as I know no one has done this, and it could scale up: a stronger model as teacher should steer more finely and elicit better, more nuanced traits (though a stronger teacher also narrows the gap, making it less weak-to-strong).
